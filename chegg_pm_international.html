Chegg International Expansion Strategy

To expand Chegg’s SaaS offering into international markets, I took a balanced approach that prioritized growth while managing operational complexity and addressing localized user needs.

Key Highlights

  • Prioritized market entry based on TAM, competitive dynamics, and LTV projections
  • Rolled out local currency presentment (LCP) and optimized regional pricing
  • Streamlined product localization with scalable tech architecture
  • Balanced regional customization with core platform consistency to maximize margin

Results

⚡ Achieved double-digit growth in new international subscriber segments within the first year of rollout.

Deep Dive: Local Currency Presentment

At Chegg, I led an initiative to improve international conversion by making our offering more relevant and accessible in key markets. We focused on deeply understanding local user needs and removing barriers to purchase. This work became critical as ChatGPT rapidly undercut our product-market fit and we needed to prove continued growth to Wall Street.

Through a program of continuous discovery that I had instituted with my designer and eng lead, we already had a pretty good idea of the international opportunity space. Customer journey mapping and funnel analysis revealed we had significant drop-off on our pricing and packaging page for international users. Improving passthrough rate on this page would drive CVR downstream.

Through customer interviews and session replay analysis we knew that a big reason that international users were dropping off here was because the page was not contextually relevant to them. The pricing was in USD, the product was priced for US purchasing power, and the value props were geared towards US customers. I recall watching user after user double click our pricing, ostensibly to go to google to convert pricing in one usability test.
